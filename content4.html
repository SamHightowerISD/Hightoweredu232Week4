Alignment

Alignment refers to the way elements are arranged in relation to each other on a page. Proper alignment in instructional design helps create a sense of order and organization, which aids in visual clarity. By aligning text, images, and interactive elements in a structured manner, designers can guide the learner’s eye through the content in a way that feels intuitive and efficient. Poor alignment can result in a disorganized layout that makes content harder to process.

In eLearning design, left-aligned text is often preferred as it supports natural reading habits in most Western languages. Consistency in alignment throughout the course content enhances the learner’s ability to navigate the material without confusion. Center alignment should be used sparingly, typically for titles or headings, while justified text should generally be avoided as it can create uneven word spacing. Overall, maintaining clear and consistent alignment is key to delivering an accessible and professional-looking learning experience.
