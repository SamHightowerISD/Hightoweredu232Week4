Color Contrast

Color contrast refers to the difference in light between foreground and background elements. It is an essential factor in instructional design because it affects readability and accessibility. High contrast between text and background improves legibility, making content easier to read. For instance, using dark text on a light background (or vice versa) creates a strong contrast, ensuring that learners can easily distinguish the content.

When designing learning materials, ensure the contrast is sufficient for learners with visual impairments, such as color blindness. Accessibility guidelines, such as the Web Content Accessibility Guidelines (WCAG), provide specific contrast ratios that designers should aim for to accommodate a broad range of users. Additionally, avoid relying solely on color to convey meaning. Use text labels or symbols to support color-coded information, which improves clarity for all learners.
