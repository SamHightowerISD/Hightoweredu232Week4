The Importance of Typography in Instructional Design

Typography plays a vital role in how learners engage with instructional content. The choice of font, font size, line spacing, and text alignment all impact readability and comprehension. Clear, readable fonts like sans-serif types are widely recommended for digital materials because they reduce eye strain. Keeping font choices consistent across a design maintains visual harmony and prevents distractions. For example, using too many different fonts can make the content seem disorganized. However, using bold or italicized text sparingly can help emphasize key points without overwhelming the learner. Proper line spacing also ensures that text is not too cluttered, making it easier for the learner to follow along.
