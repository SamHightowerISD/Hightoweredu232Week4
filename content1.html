Typography

Typography plays a crucial role in the readability and overall aesthetic of instructional materials. Good typography ensures that text is legible, attractive, and properly organized. When selecting fonts, ensure that they are appropriate for the subject matter and audience. For example, sans-serif fonts are often preferred for on-screen reading because they are more legible in digital environments. It’s also important to maintain consistency in font usage throughout the learning materials to provide a cohesive visual experience. Consider using no more than two different font types—one for headings and one for body text—to avoid cluttering the page.

Typography affects comprehension as well. Avoid using overly decorative fonts that can distract from the content. The size and spacing of text are equally important. Using appropriate line spacing and margins allows for easier reading and a cleaner layout. Ensure that font size is large enough for easy reading, especially for learners who may have visual impairments.
