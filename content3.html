White Space

White space, or negative space, refers to the unmarked areas on a page. In instructional design, white space helps create a balanced layout by preventing the page from appearing cluttered. Effective use of white space improves readability by providing visual breathing room around text and images, which makes content easier to consume.

White space also draws attention to important elements on the page, allowing learners to focus on key information without feeling overwhelmed. When creating instructional materials, consider the spacing between paragraphs, margins, and images to enhance the flow of the content.
